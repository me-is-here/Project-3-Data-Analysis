The Answer

Yes — but the effect is small. Short sleepers drink about 0.80 more cups per day than long sleepers. The relationship is real, but sleep is only one piece of why people reach for coffee.
H₀ REJECTED r² ≈ 3.6% n = 10,000

01 · The Method

Three tests, one answer

Pearson correlation for the linear relationship, Spearman as a robustness check, and a one-way ANOVA across short/normal/long sleep groups.

All three rejected H₀ at p < 0.001.

02 · The Robustness

It holds everywhere

The negative correlation between sleep and coffee appears in every subgroup tested — across genders, stress levels, and occupations.

Sleep quality shows the same pattern as sleep quantity, strengthening the result.

03 · The Caveat

Correlation ≠ causation

The data cannot say whether poor sleep drives people to coffee, or whether coffee disrupts sleep. Both are plausible.

A longitudinal study tracking individuals over time would be needed to settle the direction.
